Coming from a family of racing drivers, Max began karting at the age of six in Belgium. In 2016, he played the first Grand Prix and finished in second place. He ended the year in the fifth position and confirmed himself as one of the rarest talents in F1. The racer continued occasionally winning the Grand Prix until 2020 and improved his driving skills. However, 2021 was a big year for Verstappen, as he concluded the year as the World Champion. The racer earns millions through his award-winning races and has amassed $60 million in net worth during the span of his growing career. While the cars Max races are not street legal, he also enjoys driving British and Italian cars.

From owning a series of stunning Aston Martins to cruising in a Porsche and Mercedes, Max has a staggering collection worth millions. Let’s take a look at the most expensive cars in his garage.

Aston Martin DB11 ($208,425)

A force of speed sculpted to perfection, the DB11 is the most efficient and powerful car in the history of the DB series. As mentioned by Aston Martin, the 4.0-liter V8 twin-turbo engine gives a power output of 528 HP and 513 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle accelerates from 0 to 62 MPH in merely 4.0 seconds and has a maximum speed of 192 MPH. The two-door body style got equipped with 2 GT seats.

Aston Martin Vantage ($142,086)

The Aston Martin Vantage is an engineering marvel with agile performance and dedicated craftsmanship. The Aston Martin V12 Vantage is a powerful machine with an explosive performance, and also, the 5.2-liter V12 engine offers a top speed of 200 MPH. The interior got designed with a driver-focused cockpit equipped with semi-aniline leather seats. The Vantage focuses on weight-saving with its carbon performance seats.

Aston Martin DBS Superleggera ($334,700)

The DBS Superleggera got offered as a ragtop convertible and hardtop coupe. The sculpted hood features a V12 that cranks out 715 HP with a top speed of 211 MPH. The car comes with a leather-wrapped seat, and the trim is offered in several styles, including carbon fiber, satin-finished chrome, and ash wood, as stated by Car And Driver. Superleggera stands for Superlight, and the DBS stands true to its name as it prances from 0 to 62 MPH in merely 3.2 seconds.

Aston Martin Valkyrie ($3 million)

Launched in the golden age of hypercars, the Valkyrie has a near-open wheel design with a low-slung structure. The carbon-fiber structure offers the perfect power and weight ratio for an effortless ride. A rare edition created by Aston Martin, only 150 units of the car have been available since its launch. Ferrari Monza SP2 ($1.5 million)

A part of the Ferrari Icona series, the Monza SP2 has delved into the new series of specialized cars for the automaker. An aesthetically sculpted design, the SP2 has an interior designed for the driver. The cockpit has the instrument cluster, instruments, and seat structure designed to meet the driver’s requirements. The V12 engine powers from 0 to 62 MPH in 2.9 seconds, as noted by Ferrari.

Ferrari 488 Pista ($331,000)

Ferrari 488 Pista is a broad-shouldered drive with a graceful arch roof and bulging fenders. The two-door sports car has a 3.9-liter V8 engine under the hood that gives an output of 711 HP and 567 lb-ft of torque. It sprints from 0 to 60 MPH in 2.7 seconds. The exterior has 20-inch wheels and embellished racing stripes to give it a sporty look.

Porsche 911 GT3 RS ($188,500)

The recent iteration of the Porsche 911 GT3 RS had a facelift to stand apart from its rivals. The GT3 RS is a moto road pedigree with a 4.0-liter flat-six cylinder engine that develops 513 HP. The car has saved weight by using exotic materials to save more than 66 pounds of overall car weight.

Renault R.S.01 ($316,732)

One of the most powerful vehicles developed by Renault, the award-winning R.S.01 is a track-only racing car with a Dallara-developed carbon fiber chassis. The 3.8-liter V6 twin-turbo engine produces 550 HP and 465 lb-ft of torque, according to Top Gear. Moreover, the seven-speed sequential gearbox comes with an anti-stall function.

Mercedes-Benz C63 S AMG ($76,200)

Based on the Mercedes-Benz C-Class, the C63 S AMG is a mind-blowing automobile with a stunning appearance and equally strong aggression. The car reaches the top speed of 180 MPH in 3.7 seconds and offers a standard Mercedes-Benz interior with ample trunk space. The 4.0-liter V8 twin-turbo utilizes a nine-speed automatic transmission on the rear-wheel drive.

From being a fan of Aston Martin to experimenting with Renault and Mercedes, Max Verstappen has a mind-blowing car collection. As a big fan of speed racing, these rides offer him a race-like thrill with powerful engines that prance to top speed within seconds.
