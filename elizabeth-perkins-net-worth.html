What is Elizabeth Perkins’ Net Worth?

Elizabeth Perkins, an American actress, has a net worth of $5 million dollars. She is best known for her performances in movies like “About Last Night,” “Big,” “Avalon,” and “He Said, She Said.” Perkins has received critical acclaim for her television roles, particularly for her portrayal of Celia Hodes on the Showtime series “Weeds,” which earned her multiple Emmy and Golden Globe Award nominations. Additionally, she has appeared on several other shows including “This is Us,” “GLOW,” “Sharp Objects,” “The Moodys,” and “Truth Be Told.”

Early Life and Career

Elizabeth Perkins was born in 1960 in the Queens borough of New York City to James and Jo. She is of Greek descent on her father’s side. Perkins grew up in Colrain, Massachusetts and began acting at a young age with the community theatre group Arena Civic Theatre. She attended Northfield Mount Hermon School and later studied at DePaul University’s Goodman School of Drama in Chicago.

Perkins made her Broadway debut in 1984 in Neil Simon’s “Brighton Beach Memoirs.” She continued to work with various ensemble companies, including Steppenwolf Theater and the New York Shakespeare Festival.

Elizabeth Perkins’ Film Career

Perkins made her film debut in 1986 in “About Last Night” alongside Rob Lowe and Demi Moore. She followed up with “From the Hip” in 1987, co-starring with Judd Nelson. Her breakthrough came in 1988 with roles in “Sweet Hearts Dance” opposite Don Johnson and “Big” alongside Tom Hanks.

Elizabeth Perkins’ Career Highlights

Elizabeth Perkins had a successful year in 1990 with roles in “Love at Large,” “Enid is Sleeping,” and “Avalon.” She gained recognition for her leading roles in “He Said, She Said” and “The Doctor” in 1991. Perkins’ subsequent credits include “Indian Summer,” “The Flintstones,” “Moonlight and Valentino,” “I’m Losing You,” and “Crazy in Alabama.” In 2000, she appeared in “28 Days,” “Cats & Dogs,” and “All I Want.” She voiced Nemo’s mother in “Finding Nemo” in 2003 and appeared in “Speak” and “Jiminy Glick in Lalawood” in 2004. Perkins had a big year in 2005 with roles in “The Ring Two,” “Must Love Dogs,” and “Fierce People.” After a six-year hiatus, she returned in 2011 for “Hop.”

Elizabeth Perkins’s TV Career

Elizabeth Perkins made her television debut in 1993 in the film “For Their Own Good” and continued to appear in television films throughout the decade. In 2000, she landed her first main role in the NBC sitcom “Battery Park,” but the show was canceled after just four episodes. Perkins then returned to television films, including “What Girls Learn” in 2001 and “My Sister’s Keeper” in 2002.

From 2002 to 2004, Perkins lent her voice to “King of the Hill,” and in 2005, she appeared in two episodes of “Hercules.” Her most well-known television role came from 2005 to 2009, when she played Celia Hodes on the Showtime series “Weeds,” earning three Emmy Award nominations and two Golden Globe nominations.

After leaving “Weeds,” Perkins appeared in episodes of “Monk” and “The Closer.” She starred on the short-lived ABC sitcom “How to Live with Your Parents (For the Rest of Your Life)” in 2013, followed by appearances on “How to Get Away with Murder” and the BBC series “One Child.” She had recurring roles on “This is Us,” “GLOW,” and “Curb Your Enthusiasm.” In 2018, Perkins had a main role on the HBO miniseries “Sharp Objects,” and in 2019, she began starring on the Fox comedy “The Moodys” and the Apple TV+ series “Truth Be Told.”

Marriage and Relationships

Elizabeth Perkins tied the knot with actor and theatre director Terry Kinney in 1984. However, the couple went separate ways and got divorced in 1988. She then began dating Maurice Phillips, with whom she welcomed her daughter Hannah in 1991.

In 2000, Perkins exchanged wedding vows with Julio Macat, an Argentine-American cinematographer. The couple shares a blended family, and Perkins gained three stepsons named Alexander, Andreas, and Maximillian.
