Biography of Abigail Edna Disney

Abigail Edna Disney, born on the 24th of January 1960 in Los Angeles, California, USA, is the daughter of Roy Edward Disney and Patricia Ann Dailey, and the sister of Roy Patrick Disney. She is recognized as a philanthropist, filmmaker, film producer, and activist, particularly for documentary series on social issues. Her extensive filmography includes notable works such as “Pray the Devil Back to Hell” (2008), “The Queen of Versailles” (2012), “The Invisible War” (2012), and “Hateship, Loveship” (2013). Her involvement in the entertainment industry dates back to 2006.

Abigail Disney’s net worth is estimated to be around $500 million, primarily stemming from her involvement in films.

Abigail was raised in North Hollywood, California and pursued her education at Yale, Stanford, and Columbia University, obtaining a Bachelor’s degree, a Master’s degree in English Literature, and a Doctor of Philosophy degree, respectively.

Her career in filmmaking commenced after completing her studies. She initially produced the documentary “Pray the Devil Back to Hell” (2006), directed by Gini Reticker, which won the Best Documentary award at the Tribeca Film Festival. Together with Gini Reticker, a recipient of an Emmy Award, Abigail founded the film production company Fork Films in 2007. In this company, she held the positions of CEO, CCO, and President.

In 2011, Disney produced a five-part documentary titled “Women, War & Peace”, which received accolades such as the Television Academy Honor, the America Bar Association’s Silver Gavel Award, a Gracie Award, and the Edward R. Murrow Award from the Overseas Press Club. In recognition of her use of films to drive social change, Abigail was honored with an Athena Film Festival Award. Additionally, she served as an executive producer in several documentary films such as “The Invisible War” (2012), “This is How I Roll” (2012), “Sexy Baby” (2012), and “Alias Ruby Blade” (2012). Notably, she also worked as an executive producer on “The Trials of Spring” (2015) directed by Gini Reticker. In the same year, she made her directorial and screenwriting debut in the documentary film “The Armor of Light” (2015), which received the Best International Feature Documentary award at the Galway Film Fleadh and a Special Award at the Traverse City Film Festival.

In her personal life, Abigail Disney has been married to activist Pierre Norman Hauser since the 8th of October 1988, and the couple has four children.

She is renowned for her community activism and philanthropic work. In 1991, Abigail, along with her husband, established The Daphne Foundation, which supports individuals living in poverty. Additionally, in 2008, she founded a non-profit organization called Peace is Loud, and has been involved in various charitable events both in the USA and abroad, including the Democratic Republic of Congo.
