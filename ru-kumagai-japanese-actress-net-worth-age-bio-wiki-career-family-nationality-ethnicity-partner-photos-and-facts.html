Professional Career of Japanese Actress Ru Kumagai

Ru Kumagai began her acting career more than 15 years ago. While it is unknown how she started into acting, she made her cinematic debut in 2006 with the short film Only the Brave. Rosie Naganuma was her character.

In the beginning of her career, she went by the stage name Ruika Rose instead of her own name. After a while, she changed her stage name back to her given name. She participated in two short films in 2012, namely Got Rights? and Los Tienes?

Three years later, she appeared in another short film, Intrapersonal. The same year, she participated in Highway 31’s music video for “Miss Your Love.” Until this point, she hadn’t taken acting seriously and had done it on and off with extended pauses.

She made her television debut in 2018 with the series 9-1-1. She had a cameo role because it was her first TV appearance. She began to take acting seriously after this appearance. The following year, she starred on Adam Ruins Everything and Ryan Hansen Solves Crimes on Television.

In addition, she provided the voice of Aina in the film The Forest of Love. She was also appeared in Guy Tang’s music video for the song “Revolving Doors.” She made a brief appearance as a hostess in the TV series Good Trouble in 2021.

Following her outstanding voice acting, she provided the voice of Lee Na Yeon in the film All of Us Are Dead (2022). Her most recent appearance was as Poppy in the short film Happy Mart. She was also the producer of this short film.
