Professional Life and Career

Joseph Simmons began his career as a DJ, working with Kurtis Blow. By the mid-80s, his brother Russell had founded the musical company Def Jam. Run, together with his friend Darryl McDaniels, founded the rap group Run-D.M.C. with his brother’s encouragement. DJ Jam Master Jay joined the group in 1982, completing the legendary line-up.

The hip hop group went on to sell millions of CDs and opened a number of doors for hip-hop. They also combined rock and pop with their 1986 hit cover of Aerosmith’s “Walk This Way.” The assassination of Jam Master Jay in 2002 brought an official close to the organization. Run-DMC became the first hip hop group to attain gold certification. They went on to become the first group to obtain a Platinum-certified album, King of Rock, in 1985. Expectations were rising, as was the quality of the work. Raising Hell, their album, became the first multi-platinum record in hip hop history a year later.
